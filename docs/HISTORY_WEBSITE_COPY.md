## Main Website Version

The early history of Asingan Fire Station is closely tied to the national reorganization of public safety services in the Philippines. In `1991`, the implementation of `Republic Act No. 6975` transformed the old `PC-INP` structure and formally separated the police, fire, and jail services into the `PNP`, `BFP`, and `BJMP`. This national transition also shaped the beginnings of the Bureau of Fire Protection in Asingan.

Based on the station's current local historical account, `BFP Asingan` was already active in `1991` under `INSP Felix G. Ballesteros`. In those early years, before a dedicated station had been established, the fire unit reportedly shared office space with the `PNP`. This arrangement reflected the institutional reality of the period, as many early fire personnel were understood to have come from the old `PC-INP` era.

By `1996`, the development of a more permanent station site had begun to take clearer form. On `January 9, 1996`, `Resolution No. 4` supported the availability of a `300-square-meter` parcel of land in `Asingan, Pangasinan` for the construction of a fire station building. This was followed by the execution of a `Deed of Donation` on `February 19, 1996`, through which the `Municipality of Asingan`, represented by `Municipal Mayor Carlos D. Lopez Sr.`, donated the site to the `Bureau of Fire Protection`, represented by `Chief Inspector Rogelio C. Mejia`.

Taken together, the years from `1991` to `1996` represent the formative stage of Asingan Fire Station: a period of transition from the old `PC-INP-linked fire service setup` into the formal local identity of `BFP Asingan`, followed by the legal groundwork for a permanent station site that would support its continuing growth and service to the community.

## Short Version

`BFP Asingan traces its early identity to the 1991 reorganization of public safety services under Republic Act No. 6975. In its earliest period under INSP Felix G. Ballesteros, the unit reportedly operated from shared office space with the PNP while a dedicated station had not yet been established. By 1996, the Municipality of Asingan had formally provided a 300-square-meter site through Resolution No. 4 and a Deed of Donation, laying the foundation for the station's more permanent development under Municipal Mayor Carlos D. Lopez Sr.`

## Timeline Version

- `1991`: The implementation of `Republic Act No. 6975` reorganized the national public safety system and created the legal framework for the `PNP`, `BFP`, and `BJMP`.

- `February 19, 1996`: A `Deed of Donation` formally transferred the land to the `Bureau of Fire Protection` for the construction of the fire station building.
