# Asingan Fire Station History Draft (1991-1996)

This is a working draft of the early history of Asingan Fire Station based on the current combination of official legal context, scanned land records, and local project account. It is intentionally limited to the period from `1991` to `1996`.

## Draft Narrative

The early history of Asingan Fire Station can be understood within the national reorganization of public safety services in the Philippines. With the implementation of `Republic Act No. 6975` in `1991`, the old PC-INP public safety structure was reorganized, separating the police, fire, and jail services into the `PNP`, `BFP`, and `BJMP`. In local terms, this period marked the transition of fire service operations in Asingan into the Bureau of Fire Protection.

Based on the current project account, `BFP Asingan` was already active in `1991` under `INSP Felix G. Ballesteros`. During this early stage, when a dedicated station had not yet been established, the unit reportedly shared office space with the `PNP`. This setup fits the institutional reality of the period, since many early personnel were understood to come from the old `PC-INP` era.

By `1996`, the station's development had moved into a more permanent phase. A `300-square-meter` parcel of land in `Asingan, Pangasinan` was made available for the construction of the fire station building. The supporting local basis was `Resolution No. 4`, dated `January 9, 1996`. This was followed by the execution of a `Deed of Donation` on `February 19, 1996`, in which the `Municipality of Asingan`, represented by `Carlos D. Lopez Sr.`, donated the site to the `Bureau of Fire Protection`, represented by `Chief Inspector Rogelio C. Mejia`.

At this stage of the project, the working interpretation is that `1991 to 1996` covers the transition from the old `PC-INP-linked fire service setup` into the formal local identity of `BFP Asingan`, followed by the legal and physical groundwork for a dedicated station site.

## Working Timeline

- `1991`: National implementation of `Republic Act No. 6975` reorganized the public safety structure and created the legal framework for the `PNP`, `BFP`, and `BJMP`.
- `1991`: Based on current local account, `BFP Asingan` was active under `INSP Felix G. Ballesteros`.
- `1991`: While there was still no dedicated station, the Asingan fire unit reportedly shared office space with the `PNP`.
- `January 9, 1996`: `Resolution No. 4` supported the availability of a `300-square-meter` parcel for the fire station site.
- `February 19, 1996`: A `Deed of Donation` formally transferred the land to the `Bureau of Fire Protection` for the construction of the fire station building.

## Source Confidence

These parts are already strong:

- The national legal basis under `R.A. No. 6975`
- The 1996 land donation record
- The donor and donee representatives named in the deed

These parts are still treated as local working history and may need later support:

- The exact activation date of `BFP Asingan` in `1991`
- The shared-office arrangement with the `PNP`
- The fuller list of early personnel from the `PC-INP` era
- The exact construction completion timeline of the old fire station

## Best Current Short Version

`BFP Asingan traces its early institutional identity to the 1991 reorganization of public safety services under Republic Act No. 6975. In its earliest period under INSP Felix G. Ballesteros, the unit reportedly operated from shared office space with the PNP while a dedicated station had not yet been established. By 1996, the municipality had formally provided a 300-square-meter site through Resolution No. 4 and a Deed of Donation, laying the foundation for the station's more permanent development under Municipal Mayor Carlos D. Lopez Sr.`
