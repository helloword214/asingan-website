## Working Narrative

Asingan Fire Station did not become active in emergency medical response only after receiving newer transport assets. Based on the current station account, the station was already actively helping in `vehicular accident response` by `2015` under `FINSP Loreto M. Bernardino`. At that time, BFP personnel with nursing background were already assisting together with the `LGU ambulance`, and EMS supplies were supported by the `LGU`, showing that EMS had already become part of the station's practical service role.

The station's EMS identity appears to have grown even stronger when the `L300` was turned over to the station. In the current working account, this period is remembered as a time when `BFP Asingan became more recognized for transporting patients`, expanding its visibility in emergency response beyond fire suppression.

Later, with the arrival of the `Toyota Japan ambulance`, the station's EMS role reportedly reached a more intense and sustained level. According to the current account, annual EMS responses were `close to or not below 100 per year`, making EMS response one of the notable operational identities of Asingan Fire Station.

## Website-Ready Version

Beyond fire suppression, Asingan Fire Station also developed a strong role in emergency medical response. By `2015`, under the leadership of `FINSP Loreto M. Bernardino`, the station was already actively assisting in `vehicular accident response`, working alongside the `LGU ambulance`, supported by BFP personnel with nursing background, and backed by `LGU-supported EMS supplies`.

The station's EMS presence grew further when the `L300` was turned over to the station, helping make `BFP Asingan more recognized for patient transport` within the community. Later, with the arrival of the `Toyota Japan ambulance`, EMS operations reportedly expanded even more, with annual EMS responses remaining `close to or not below 100 per year`.

Taken together, these developments show that EMS response became an important operational identity of Asingan Fire Station, reflecting a broader commitment not only to fire protection, but also to emergency medical assistance and patient transport.
