# Website Foundation

This document defines the key information that the Asingan Fire Station website should contain before or during the initial build. It also locks the direction of the site as `blog-ready` from the start.

## Core Direction

The website should be built as:

- An official institutional website
- A future-ready `News and Stories` publishing platform

This means we are not building a simple one-time brochure page. We are building a site that can later grow into a content-driven public information platform without changing its core structure.

## Recommended Site Map

- `Home`
- `About / Station Profile`
- `History`
- `Fire Marshals / Leadership`
- `Organization Structure`
- `Assets and Apparatus`
- `EMS and Rescue`
- `Services`
- `News and Stories`
- `Gallery`
- `Contact / Emergency Information`

## Blog-Ready Plan

The website should support a future publishing workflow under a section such as:

- `News and Stories`

Recommended future content types:

- `Announcements`
- `Operations and Accomplishments`
- `Fire Safety Tips`
- `Community Activities`
- `EMS Stories`
- `Historical Features`

## Suggested Content Architecture

- `data/` for structured station records
- `content/posts/` for future article-style content

Examples of structured records that belong in `data/`:

- Fire marshals
- Vehicles
- EMS milestones
- Land records
- History events
- Organization structure

Examples of publishing content that should later live in `content/posts/`:

- News updates
- Safety advisories
- Event recaps
- Station achievements
- Community outreach stories

## Homepage Planning Rule

The homepage should not only introduce the station. It should also be ready to surface future published content.

That means the homepage should eventually have space for:

- `Featured history`
- `Featured apparatus`
- `EMS highlight`
- `Latest news and stories`
- `Contact and emergency quick actions`

## Immediate Documentation Priorities

Before heavy implementation, the highest-value information to finalize is:

1. `Organization structure`
2. `Station profile`
3. `Services`
4. `Contact and emergency details`
5. `Homepage content direction`
